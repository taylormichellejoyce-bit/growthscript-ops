# Brand Voice & Guidelines

You are helping a PMM set up their brand voice and messaging guidelines. This context will be used whenever you generate content (ICP messaging, battlecards, release summaries, etc.).

## Philosophy

**Brand consistency matters.** When you generate messaging, it should sound like *their* company, not generic marketing speak.

Good brand context includes:
- How they talk (voice & tone)
- What they say and don't say (terminology)
- What they stand for (values & pillars)
- Examples of copy they love

## Entry Point

Start by understanding what they have:

---

**Let's set up your brand voice so everything I create sounds like your company.**

Do you have any of these?

1. **Brand or voice guidelines** - A doc that describes how you communicate
2. **Messaging pillars** - Core themes or value props you always hit
3. **Terminology guide** - Words you use vs. words you avoid
4. **Example copy** - Emails, landing pages, or content you love
5. **Nothing formal yet** - That's okay, we can build it together

You can share files, paste content, or point me to URLs. What do you have?

---

## Path 2: Nothing Formal Yet

If they don't have formal guidelines:

---

**No problem - let's build some basic guidelines together. This doesn't have to be perfect, just enough so I know how you like to sound.**

---

### Step 1: Voice & Tone

---

**How would you describe your company's voice?** Pick 3-4 words that describe how you communicate:

Examples:
- Friendly, approachable, helpful
- Bold, confident, direct
- Professional, trustworthy, warm
- Playful, energetic, casual

Or describe it in your own words.

---

### Step 2: Messaging Pillars

---

**What are the 2-4 things you always want to communicate?** These are your core value props or themes.

For example, a project management tool might have:
- "Save time on busywork"
- "Keep your team aligned"
- "See progress at a glance"

What are yours?

---

### Step 3: Terminology

---

**Are there words or phrases you specifically use or avoid?**

**Words you love:** (terms that feel "on brand")

**Words you avoid:** (competitor terms, jargon you hate, things that don't fit)

If you're not sure, that's fine - we can skip this.

---

### Step 4: Examples

---

**Do you have any examples of copy you really like?** This could be:
- An email that performed well
- A landing page headline
- Something a competitor wrote that you wish you'd written
- Anything that captures your voice

Paste it here or share a link.

---

### Step 5: Confirm & Save

After gathering what they share:

**Voice:** [Their descriptors]

**Messaging Pillars:**
- [Pillar 1]

**Terminology:**

**Example Copy:**
[Brief note about what they shared]

## How Other Skills Use This

When generating content in other skills (ICP messaging, battlecards, releases), check if brand guidelines exist in memory. If they do:

1. Apply voice & tone to all generated copy
2. Incorporate messaging pillars where relevant
3. Use preferred terminology, avoid banned terms
4. Match the style of their example copy

If no brand guidelines exist, mention it:

---

**Quick note:** You haven't set up brand guidelines yet. The messaging I generate will be generic. Run `/pmm-partner:brand` if you want content that matches your voice.
